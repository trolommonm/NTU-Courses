BH2602 TOTAL REWARDS MANAGEMENT 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB103 & AB105 OR
AB1202 & AB1601
Mutually exclusive with: BH223
This course will cover the contemporary theories and practices on Total Rewards Management from the perspectives of HR professionals. It will primarily focus on the process of designing an internally aligned and externally competitive total rewards package to attract, retain and engage talents. Besides this, the course will also cover topics like, performance management, Work-Life Balance, expatriate compensation, and other related issues that might impact on total rewards related decisions.
 
BH2604 MANAGING & CONSULTING THROUGH RESEARCH 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB103 & AB105 OR
AB1202 & AB1601
Mutually exclusive with: AB213, BH225
This course is designed to help students understand and implement techniques that underlie research on the management of human resources in organizations. Research plays an integral role in the management of people in today's organizations. Companies value talent with the skills to apply scientific research practices to help improve employee productivity and well-being. Similarly, many organizations also engage external consultants to help solve problems with their workforce via research. This course will equip students with the knowledge, skills, and abilities to function as both internal and external research consultants. The course begins with discussions of the principles of scientific research, and how they can be applied in a HR context. We then progress to cover the various aspects of designing research to investigate HR-related issues. Students will then learn to use statistical analysis to inform understanding of HR phenomena. The course finally ends with discussions and activities designed to help students report and present research findings.
 
BH3301 EMPLOYMENT LAW 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: AB107 OR
AB1301
Employment Law is critical to managing human capital. This course focuses on the employment law in Singapore as a foundation for an understanding of the employment relationship and its incidents. Some of these issues and problems include drafting employment contracts, the minimum mandatory protection for employees, wrongful or unfair dismissals, and work safety and compensation for injuries caused at work. Besides a sound working grasp of the basic legal principles, the course is also intended to provide perspectives, theoretical and practical, from which to critically assess the possible solutions and the law's responses to vital problems commonly encountered both in Singapore and abroad. A selected few topics are given a comparative international treatment.
 
BH3602 HR TECHNOLOGY, METRICS & PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT 4.0 AU
Prerequisite: BH223 & BH226 OR
BH2601 & BH2602
Mutually exclusive with: BH326
This course has two distinct components- HR Costing and Performance Management. HR Costing highlights how people management affects the organizational bottom line. This course will provide students illustrations on how to calculate the costs of human behaviour in an organization using various current methodologies. Students will also engage in practical assignments that develop their professional competencies in developing and managing HR costing system. Performance Management covers the issues related to the strategic development and choices of the system that enhances employee performance and contribution to organizational effectiveness. Students will develop an awareness of strategies and methods in managing an on-going evaluation and development systems with other HR functions.

Prerequisite: AB105 OR

Not available as Core to Programme: MS-2ndMaj/Spec(ITG)(2016-onwards), MS-2ndMaj/Spec(MSB)(2016-onwards)
This course will emphasize on the importance of the impact of culture on managing people in today's global business environment. It seeks to develop the knowledge and skills needed to manage effectively in different cultural settings, and to work effectively with people from other cultures. Specifically, the course will develop an awareness of the pervasive and hidden influence of culture on human behavior,particularly with respect to various management philosophies and practices. It will cover issues of cross-cultural communication, negotiation, international teamwork, workforce diversity, and help develop the interaction skills necessary for today's international managers in dealing with people from different national and cultural boundaries. A major focus of the course will be on the concepts and models of international human resource management and development, that are relevant to the management of multinational enterprises (MNEs) and international organizations.
